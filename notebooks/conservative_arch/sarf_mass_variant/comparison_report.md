## 4. Interpretation

### 4.1 LM quality: the surprisal prior wins decisively

At identical parameter count (within $d+1=129$), identical
optimiser, schedule, data and seed, the four variants land at
val perplexities `287 -> 192 -> 223 -> 161`.  SARF + logfreq
mass (B) is the best configuration by a clear margin:
~**44%** ppl reduction relative to fixed-$\xi$ SPLM, and
~**17%** ppl reduction relative to the SARF-faithful variant
that already won the previous ablation round.  By contrast,
SARF + embed-head mass (A) *loses* ~16% ppl to plain SARF.

The asymmetry between (A) and (B) is the real finding.
Both variants have essentially the same expressive capacity
in principle (the embed-head linear projection can implement
any per-token scalar function of the embedding, and should be
strictly richer than a frozen surprisal lookup).  Yet at
Tiny Shakespeare scale the theoretically-motivated prior
beats the data-driven learner.  The most natural reading is
an **inductive-bias-vs-data-efficiency** story: logfreq tells
the optimiser "rare tokens are heavier" from step 0, fixing
the *shape* of the mass so only a single scale $\alpha$ has
to be learned; embed-head must discover both the shape and
the scale from 300 K tokens of Shakespeare.  The prior
happens to be approximately right -- content/rare tokens
*are* the ones you want to anchor -- so the prior does most
of the work and the remaining tunable $\alpha \approx 0.1$
suffices.

This is the clearest quantitative evidence so far that the
framework's information-theoretic reading of semantic mass
($\semm_t \propto -\log p(x_t)$) is not just allegorical.
It is *prescriptive*: the observed 17% ppl gain over the
single-scalar SARF baseline comes for a single extra scalar
parameter and a frozen vocabulary-sized lookup table.

### 4.2 Depth-axis shared-$V_\psi$: logfreq also closes the
gap that SARF had opened

The SARF-faithful ablation had introduced a modest structural
cost: pooled depth-direction TEST $R^2$ dropped from +0.79
(fixed-$\xi$) to +0.71 (SARF), with the framework reading
that a pointwise $V_\psi(h)$ cannot capture the sequence-
level functional SARF implements.  Per-token mass changes
this picture:

- SARF + embed-head (A): pooled TEST $R^2$ = +0.72, essentially
  the same as plain SARF, but the worst-layer $R^2$ climbs from
  +0.48 to +0.57, i.e. the profile becomes more uniform.
- SARF + logfreq (B): pooled TEST $R^2$ = **+0.84**, *better
  than fixed-$\xi$ SPLM* (+0.79).  No layer drops below +0.67;
  median per-layer +0.87.

Variant (B) is therefore doing two things at once: (i) lower
validation cross-entropy, and (ii) higher pointwise shared-$V_\psi$
fidelity along the depth axis.  The combination is remarkable
because the two metrics a priori trade off against each other
-- LM quality usually pulls toward expressive, non-conservative
dynamics, while shared-$V_\psi$ fidelity pulls toward strict
pointwise conservativity.  The logfreq mass finds a regime where
both improve simultaneously.

Framework reading: the surprisal-shaped mass gives different
tokens different effective step sizes in the integrator
($v_t = (v_t + dt f_t / m_t) / (1 + dt\gamma)$), and this
factorises cleanly into a per-token scale that the shared
$V_\psi(h)$ fit can absorb through its per-layer $\alpha_\ell, \beta_\ell$
constants (the fit is still strictly pointwise in $h$).
In other words, logfreq mass does not add sequence-level
coupling (that is what SARF $\xi$ re-pooling does); it just
*tilts* the integrator toward heavier anchoring of rare tokens,
which happens to regularise the dynamics back into the shared-
scalar family.

### 4.3 Token-direction: gradual softening, no collapse

Along the token axis the picture is monotonic in the other
direction: pooled TEST $R^2$ drops `0.52 -> 0.41 -> 0.36 -> 0.33`
across fixed-$\xi$ / SARF / SARF+A / SARF+B.  Adding SARF
$\xi$ re-pooling costs ~0.11, per-token mass costs an
additional ~0.05--0.08.  No variant collapses, and no layer
in any variant goes below +0.195.  All four stay firmly in
SPLM-like territory, at least an order of magnitude above
the GPT-2 middle-band near-zero values from §14.4.

This is exactly the pattern we would expect theoretically:
every step that introduces more cross-token coupling (xi
re-pooling; per-token mass) makes the pointwise shared-$V_\psi$
fit harder along the token axis where the coupling lives.
Closing these gaps is the job of a $\xi$- and $m$-aware
$V_\psi(\xi, h, m)$ fit (Q8 in the paper).

### 4.4 What we learnt about semantic mass

1. **Per-token mass has empirical content on top of SARF
    $\xi$ re-pooling.**  One of the two variants beats SARF on
    LM quality, and *both* variants produce non-trivial per-
    token dispersion (std 0.16--0.21 around means 0.84--1.28).
2. **The framework's surprisal prior is better than a free
    learned head at this scale.**  The gain from fixing the
    shape of $\semm_t$ to $-\log\hat p(x_t)$ is ~17% ppl over
    SARF while adding a single scalar parameter.  The free
    linear head underperforms, presumably because 4000 steps
    on 300 K tokens is not enough to learn the right shape
    from scratch.
3. **Per-token mass improves the depth-axis shared-$V_\psi$
    separator** (logfreq reaches +0.84 > fixed-$\xi$ +0.79),
    showing that the conservative-by-construction narrative
    *tightens* when we implement more of the framework, not
    loosens.  This is the opposite of what we saw when moving
    from fixed-$\xi$ to SARF-$\xi$ alone.
4. **The concatenation of SARF $\xi$ and logfreq mass is
    where the framework lives.**  On Tiny Shakespeare,
    implementing both prescriptions simultaneously gives the
    best LM quality, the cleanest depth-axis conservativity,
    and still only ~0.19 lower token-axis $R^2$ than the
    fixed-$\xi$ variant -- at ~44% lower perplexity.

### 4.5 Open questions

- **Does embed-head (A) eventually match logfreq (B)?**  Either
  via longer training, larger corpus, or mass-head
  regularisation toward the surprisal profile?  If yes, the
  prior is a useful accelerator but not a structural choice;
  if no, the prior encodes something the linear head cannot
  learn from a single scalar of signal per token.
- **Context-aware $V_\psi(\xi, h, m)$ fit.**  The pointwise
  shared-$V_\psi(h)$ used here cannot see $\xi$ or $m$
  directly.  A context-aware oracle should drive the token-axis
  R^2 back toward fixed-$\xi$ levels; quantifying the gap
  closes the Q8 storyline.
- **Scale-up.**  All the above is at 7.1 M params on 300 K
  tokens.  The framework's claim is that the conservative-
  by-construction circuit is *inference-efficient*, not that
  it wins at every corpus size.  The next controlled step is
  to repeat the four-way on TinyStories and WikiText.
